Latis Intelligence Platform

App development roadmap · June 2026 — February 2027

Nine stages take Latis Intelligence Platform from current state through external brand-direct pilot and Phase 2 activation paths for the other use cases by February 2027. Latis is the unified platform; the Sales Deck Generator is one feature within it, used by Spectrum reps to produce client-facing sales decks. At Stage 6 (RBAC, October 2026) the app forks into two tracks on the same codebase. The Internal Spectrum track gets UC4 + UC6 — the app plus the Sales Deck Generator. The External Brand-Direct track gets UC4 + UC1, UC2, UC3, UC5 — the app (SDG hidden) plus the four downstream activation paths the brand pushes into: Spectrum Reach managed service via RADR (UC5), clean-room collaboration (UC2), DSP / model training (UC1), and agentic / MCP (UC3). The fork is governed entirely by the role layer; everything below it stays unified.

Stages 1 through 4 all complete by end of July so Spectrum pilot and internal feedback begins August 1, 2026. Stages 2 and 3 run concurrently from July onward — Stage 3 (feature lock + DoD signoff + LLM grounding) runs longer than Stage 2 (AWS migration). Stages 7 and 8 run concurrently from November 2026 onward — Stage 8 (Brand-Direct Pilot) runs longer than Stage 7 (Phase 1 data-dependent features). Two continuous streams — user testing and QA — run alongside every stage. Five critical milestones gate the program.

The six Latis use cases Latis Intelligence Platform serves six go-to-market use cases, ordered by build priority. UC4 is the platform itself — the app, and the front door to everything else. UC6 (Internal Sales Deck Generation) is the main commercial output Spectrum reps produce from the app and is the centerpiece of the Spectrum pilot. UC5 / UC2 / UC1 / UC3 are the four downstream activation paths the External Brand-Direct track exports into, in that priority order.

Definition of Done · App launch readiness Plain-English checklist of what "done" looks like for the current app and its activation expansion. Built on Latis_DoD_and_Context_Pack_v2 and extended for the platform-wide build.

Data
  • Running on full data, not the 1% sample (or extrapolation from the sample is disclosed inline wherever used).
  • Foundations-team de-sampled numbers in place across every page.
  • No hardcoded placeholder or zero values anywhere on a user-facing surface.
  • Source label with as-of date on every page that shows numbers.
Numbers and consistency
  • Same number means the same thing everywhere. Brand reach on Your Audience matches reach on Insights matches reach on Your Best Play matches whatever appears on the Sales Deck — to the digit.
  • One source of truth per KPI. No page recomputes a number another page already produces.
  • Every KPI has a reconciliation query an analyst can run against the data layer and match.
  • Cross-page consistency verified for all curated brands before launch.
App behavior
  • All seven pages render: Welcome, Select Brand, Your Audience, Insights, Your Best Play, Summary, Sales Deck.
  • Brand selection works two ways: curated brand cards and free-form domain entry with optional campaign-objective field.
  • Curated brand library is admin-managed via the Curate Brands surface; brands can be added or retired without code change.
  • Each selected brand produces a complete experience across Your Audience, Insights, Best Play, Summary, and Sales Deck.
  • Live query runs once on first brand load and caches the result; sub-second navigation after initial analysis.
  • Ask Latis is available globally as a slide-over panel from any analysis page.
  • App works in a sales meeting: tolerant of single LLM failure, predictable load times, no broken or empty states.
Sales Deck tab
  • Renders the deck as currently constituted, pulling dynamic numbers from the data (or extrapolating from 1% sample where direct full-footprint values aren't available).
  • Generates the same use cases on the Your Best Play cards in the deck where appropriate.
  • Every number in the deck reconciles to the same number shown on the corresponding analysis page.
  • Use-case content matches the use case the app recommended for that brand on Your Best Play.
  • Extrapolation methodology disclosed inline on the slide when 1% sample is used.
  • Deck generation is a one-time live query, cached after first run for the selected brand.
LLM output
  • Every LLM output is grounded in data we actually have — no invented numbers, no made-up competitor activity, no speculative claims.
  • Approved Latis positioning language appears verbatim wherever referenced. No paraphrasing.
  • No claims that violate client confidentiality, surface PII, or expose individual household behavior.
  • Graceful degradation: when the LLM is unavailable, analysis pages still function on canned, data-grounded insights ranked by signal strength alone.
  • Output is consistent across repeated runs for the same brand. No volatility that would surprise a rep mid-meeting.
  • Ask Latis answers grounded in real data — no hallucinated metrics, no fabricated competitor claims.
Performance and reliability
  • Pages load within a few seconds for any brand after the first cached analysis.
  • First-time live query completes within an acceptable wait window — predictable, visibly progressing, not hanging.
  • App stays up during sales hours. No multi-minute cold-start delays.
  • Auth in place (sign-in is required on the current app).
  • Session state is server-side and shareable, not browser-local.
Branding and compliance
  • Latis brand identity applied throughout. No Spectrum Reach or Charter consumer brand bleed.
  • Legal and brand owner have reviewed and signed off on every client-facing surface, including the generated deck.
  • Latis positioning language reviewed and approved.
Sign-offs
  • Named pilot users have used the app end-to-end against all curated brands and approved.
  • Charter Foundations team has approved the numbers.
  • Latis brand owner has approved the copy.
  • Legal review complete.

Latis Context Pack · portable brief for AI tools Paste this into the system prompt of any LLM or AI agent that needs to understand Latis. Self-contained; assumes the reader knows nothing about Charter, Spectrum Reach, or Latis. Extended with the six use cases.

1
What Latis is

Latis is Charter Communications' behavioral intelligence platform. It turns the household-level browsing, device, and video signals already flowing across Charter's network into structured audience insights that advertisers can use. The product is built and operated by Charter Intelligence Ventures (CIV), an internal Charter business unit. Latis is sold to brand advertisers and to the agencies that buy media on their behalf.

Tagline: Intelligence. Alive.

2
The data underneath

Latis runs on three first-party Charter signal sources, all collected passively at the household level, with no surveys and no panels.

  • Domain browsing (Deepfield DNS) — what households visit on the open internet. Approximately 30–31.5 million households in the current footprint, scanning roughly 0.6 trillion DNS events per day across the universe.
  • Network and device telemetry (CUJO) — what connected devices are in the home and how they behave. Approximately 486M+ connected devices observed across the footprint.
  • Video viewership — linear and streaming viewing patterns. Approximately 91B+ video consumption events per year. Integration is in progress; full availability is a Phase 1A target.

All three are deterministic signals collected from the network itself. None of them comes from a survey, an opt-in panel, or a modeled lookalike. Charter's footprint covers 41 states.

3
What makes Latis different

Four claims define Latis's positioning. Every one of them is grounded in how the data is collected, not in branding choice.

  • Deterministic, not probabilistic. Every signal is a real observed behavior at a real household, not an estimate.
  • Household-level, not device-level. The unit of analysis is the home, so every device in the home contributes to one behavioral profile.
  • Always-on, not survey-based. Continuous network observation. No recall bias, no opt-in panel.
  • Pre-purchase intent, not post-transaction history. The data shows what households are doing before they decide.
4
Approved positioning language · verbatim, no paraphrase

These two statements are the official Latis positioning. They appear verbatim in any context where Latis describes its privacy stance, identity handling, or competitive position. No synonyms, no restructuring, no qualifiers.

"We transform signals, we do not expose behavior. We enable relevance without identity."
"Google = intent (search). Meta = affinity (like). Amazon = transaction (bought). Latis = behavior (what households are doing across the full home environment, before decisions)."
5
What Latis never says

Hard rules. Anything that violates them is suppressed before reaching a client. They apply to every output in every channel, including any LLM-generated content.

  • No personally identifiable information. No names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, household IDs, or device identifiers.
  • No claim about a specific named individual's behavior. Latis sees households and segments, not people.
  • No reference to a Latis client other than the one currently being discussed. No cross-engagement leak.
  • No invented numbers. Every figure is traceable to a query against the real data, a stated assumption with disclosed methodology, or a cited external source.
  • No "will" or "guaranteed" attached to campaign outcomes. Latis describes what is observed, not what will happen.
  • No demographic or behavioral claim about a client's audience that the client could already get from their own CRM or loyalty system, unless the index is at least 1.2x against the Charter average and the skew is disclosed.
  • No paraphrase of the positioning language above.
  • No mixing of Latis brand identity with Spectrum Reach or Charter consumer brands. Latis is distinct.
6
Tone and voice

Latis sounds like a senior analyst delivering a finding, not a salesperson making a pitch. Claims are stated declaratively when the data supports them, qualified honestly when support is partial, and omitted entirely when support is absent. Hedging is reserved for genuine uncertainty.

Latis is technically precise. It uses the right words for the right concepts (deterministic, behavioral, household-level, passive, signal). It avoids marketing fluff (game-changing, next-generation, AI-powered).

7
Vocabulary
  • Household (HH) — the unit of analysis. A single Charter-subscribed home with all its devices.
  • Signal — an observed behavior. A domain visit, a device fingerprint, a viewing event.
  • Vertical — a behavioral category. Retail & Shopping, Financial Services, Automotive, Sportsbook, Fitness, SVOD, CPG / Loyalty, and similar industry groupings.
  • Index — comparison to the Charter population average. 100 = same as average. 120 = 20% above average. Used everywhere Latis describes audience skew.
  • Co-visit — two domains visited by the same household. The foundation of cross-category insight.
  • Intender — a household showing in-market behavioral signals for a category (e.g., travel intender, auto intender).
  • Embeddings — high-dimensional vector representation of a household's behavioral state, generated by the Latis embedding model and pre-calculated daily.
  • Clean Room — Latis's Snowflake-based data collaboration environment. Partner first-party data is matched and enriched against Latis Intelligence Features within Latis's governed compute zone.
  • Blockgraph — the identity spine used by Latis to manage household identifiers. Spectrum Reach operates on Blockgraph natively.
  • MCP (Model Context Protocol) — the standard for connecting AI agents to external data sources. Latis exposes Intelligence Features via MCP for agentic workflow use cases.
  • Phase 0 / Phase 1A — current state vs enhanced state. Phase 0 covers approximately 15% of high-intent domain activity (current sampling). Phase 1A covers approximately 30% US household coverage (full footprint, post Snowflake provisioning).
8
What Latis is for

Latis is built to support a sales conversation between a Charter / Spectrum Reach rep and an advertiser (brand or agency). The product surfaces audience intelligence the advertiser cannot get elsewhere, organized into a persuasive narrative tailored to that advertiser. The goal of every Latis output is to give the rep a moment where the client says some version of "we think about you differently now."

Latis is not a media-buying tool, not a campaign-management platform, and not a measurement system. It is an intelligence layer that informs the conversation.

  • UC6 · Internal Sales Deck Generation — SDG (Priority 2). Spectrum reps pull intelligence out of Latis and push it into the Sales Deck Generator to produce client-facing sales decks for advertisers. The main commercial output of the Internal Spectrum track and the anchor of the Spectrum pilot. Buyer: internal Spectrum reps. Gated to the Spectrum role after RBAC.
  • UC5 · Managed Service via Spectrum Reach + RADR (Priority 3). Latis builds the audiences; Spectrum Reach runs campaigns on Charter linear + CTV inventory via Freewheel / RADR. First activation path on the External Brand-Direct track. Buyer: brands and agencies purchasing through Spectrum Reach.
  • UC2 · Data Collaboration / Clean Room (Priority 4). Snowflake / Blockgraph zero-copy environment where partners bring 1P data; we enrich with Intelligence Features. Dependency: clean room must already be set up. Buyer: RMN product teams + agency partners. Example: Walmart Connect.
  • UC1 · AI Model Training (Priority 5). Latis Embeddings — daily-refreshed household behavioral vectors — exported via API microservice (sub-50ms p99) or daily bulk S3 push. Trains DSP bidding models, recommendation engines, LLMs. Buyer: DSP ML / data engineering, AI model teams. Example: Amazon DSP, Anthropic.
  • UC3 · Agentic Workflow Enhancement / MCP (Priority 6). Latis Intelligence Features exposed via Model Context Protocol for AI agents to query at decision time. Buyer: AI product / platform engineering teams.

UC6 is the primary commercial output of the Internal Spectrum track. UC5/UC2/UC1/UC3 are the activation paths the External Brand-Direct track exports into.
